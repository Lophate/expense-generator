1. Purpose

This SOP establishes the policies and procedures for travel-related expense reimbursement at Ad Astra Group. It ensures that business travel expenses are incurred responsibly, documented properly, and reimbursed in a timely and consistent manner.

All team members are expected to exercise sound judgment and select the most economical options that meet legitimate business needs.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to all Ad Astra Group team members, contractors, and authorized representatives who incur travel expenses on behalf of the organization for domestic and international business travel.

It covers airfare, lodging, meals, ground transportation, mileage, and incidentals. It does not cover routine local expenses unrelated to business travel (for example, daily commuting costs).

3. Responsibilities / Roles

Role Responsibilities
Founder (Anhthu Nguyen) Approves all travel requests prior to booking. Reviews and approves reimbursement submissions. Authorizes exceptions to standard reimbursement limits.
Administrative Business Partner Processes reimbursement submissions. Verifies receipts and GSA rate compliance.
Traveling Team Member Obtains pre-approval for all travel. Books travel in accordance with this SOP. Retains all itemized receipts. Submits reimbursement requests within 30 calendar days of trip completion.

4. Prerequisites / Required Resources

  • Written pre-approval for the trip (via email or Google Chat)
  • Access to the GSA per diem rate lookup tool: gsa.gov/travel/plan-book/per-diem-rates
  • Access to the State Department foreign per diem rate lookup: aoprals.state.gov/web920/per_diem.asp
  • Venmo account (for contractors) or direct deposit enrollment (for W-2 employees)
  • Itemized receipts for all expenses claimed

5. Procedure (Main Steps)

Pre-Approval

  • All business travel must be pre-approved by Anhthu before any bookings are made.
  • Submit a travel request via email or Google Chat including destination, travel dates, purpose of trip, and estimated total cost.
  • Approval must be documented in retrievable written form (email or Google Chat). Verbal approvals are not sufficient.
  • Do not book any travel until written approval has been received.
  • Exception: If Anhthu directly tasks travel in writing (email, Google Chat, or Asana), that written directive serves as pre-approval. Retain that directive with reimbursement documentation.

Airfare

  • All flights must be booked in economy class unless otherwise approved in writing by Anhthu prior to purchase.
  • Team members may book through any airline or booking platform.
  • Select reasonably priced itineraries. Premium economy, business class, and first class are not reimbursable without prior written approval.
  • Baggage fees are reimbursable only when checked baggage contains business-required items. Personal baggage fees are not reimbursable unless travel exceeds 5 days.

Lodging

  • Hotel room charges (excluding taxes and mandatory fees) must not exceed the applicable GSA lodging per diem for destination and dates.
  • Domestic travel uses GSA rates. International travel uses U.S. State Department foreign per diem rates.
  • If a hotel exceeds the GSA maximum, obtain written approval from Anhthu before booking with justification (event-designated hotel, limited availability, safety concerns).
  • Without prior overage approval, Ad Astra Group reserves the right to reimburse only up to the GSA maximum lodging rate.
  • Taxes and mandatory hotel fees are reimbursable separately and do not count toward the GSA room cap.

Meals and Incidental Expenses (M&IE)

  • Meal and incidental allowances follow GSA M&IE rates (domestic) or State Department rates (international).
  • If meals are provided by a conference, event, client, or hotel, deduct the corresponding meal amount from that day's allowance.
  • Example: If total M&IE is $79/day and dinner is valued at $26, then only $53 may be claimed for that day.
  • Itemized meal receipts are required. Credit card statements alone are insufficient.
  • Alcohol is never reimbursable. If alcohol appears on a receipt, it must be excluded from reimbursement.

Ground Transportation

  • Rideshare (Uber, Lyft, etc.) must use the most economical tier. Premium tiers are not reimbursable without documented, pre-approved business need.
  • Rental vehicles must be the most economical class meeting business needs. Upgrades for personal preference are non-reimbursable.
  • Personal vehicle use is reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate ($0.70 per mile for 2025). Provide odometer readings or a mileage app log.
  • Parking fees and tolls are reimbursable with receipts. Due to mailing delays, tolls may be filed after the standard 30-day filing window.

International Travel

  • All policies in this SOP apply equally to international travel.
  • Lodging and M&IE rates follow the U.S. State Department foreign per diem schedule.
  • Currency conversion is based on the exchange rate on each transaction date. Use credit card or bank statement rates, or a verifiable source such as xe.com.
  • If company-directed international travel requires a passport or visa, Ad Astra Group reimburses required application fees for that trip.

Cancellations and Changes

  • Changes or cancellations required by business needs are fully reimbursable.
  • Changes for personal preference or convenience require prior approval from Anhthu or fees are non-reimbursable.

Combining Personal and Business Travel

  • If personal days are added to a business trip, only the originally approved or directed business days are reimbursable.
  • The team member is responsible for incremental costs from personal extensions, including airfare differences, extra hotel nights, non-business-day meals, and transportation.
  • The personal extension must not interfere with scheduled business obligations.

Receipt and Documentation Requirements

  • Itemized receipts are required for every claimed expense, regardless of amount.
  • For meals, the receipt must list ordered items; a total-only charge slip is insufficient.
  • For mileage, provide date, origin, destination, miles driven, and business purpose.
  • Incomplete or missing documentation may delay or deny reimbursement.

Submission and Reimbursement

  • Submit reimbursement requests within 30 calendar days after trip completion.
  • Include a consolidated summary by category, itemized receipts, destination per diem documentation, and the pre-approval communication transcript.
  • Reimbursement method: contractors via Venmo during contract period; W-2 employees via ACH direct deposit through payroll.
  • Reimbursements are processed within 14 business days after a complete and approved submission.

Expected timeline: 15 to 30 minutes for submission preparation, then up to 14 business days for processing.

6. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are not reimbursable unless specifically approved in writing by Anhthu prior to being incurred:

  • Alcohol, including during business meals
  • Personal entertainment (movies, sightseeing, recreational activities)
  • Flight upgrades (premium economy, business class, first class)
  • Spouse, partner, or companion travel expenses
  • Traffic violations, parking tickets, or moving violations
  • Laundry or dry cleaning (unless trip exceeds 5 consecutive days)
  • In-room movies, hotel minibar charges, spa services, or personal amenity fees
  • Pet boarding, pet care, or house-sitting costs
  • Childcare costs incurred due to travel
  • Personal phone charges beyond normal business use
  • Lost or stolen personal property
  • Travel insurance purchased independently (unless pre-approved)
  • Premium rideshare tiers (Black, SUV, Lux, Comfort, XL)
  • No-show fees caused by personal reasons
  • Gift shop purchases or souvenirs
  • Personal baggage fees (unless bag contains required business materials)

7. Exceptions and Escalations

  • Hotel rate exceeds GSA maximum: Must be approved in writing by Anhthu before booking. If not pre-approved, reimbursement may be capped to applicable GSA rate.
  • Non-economy airfare: Must be pre-approved in writing with justification (for example, no economy seats available or medical accommodation).
  • Lost receipts: Submit written explanation with date, vendor, amount, and business purpose. Approval without receipts is discretionary and not guaranteed.
  • Reimbursement submitted after 30 days: Late submissions require explanation and approval and are not guaranteed when submitted more than 60 days after trip completion.
  • Emergency expenses: Document as thoroughly as possible and include explanation for case-by-case review.
